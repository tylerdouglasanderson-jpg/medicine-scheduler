1What this tool does

This app builds an optimal monthly inpatient-medicine call schedule using an exact solver — not a heuristic, not a greedy fill. It runs entirely in your browser: nothing you type is uploaded anywhere, and once the page is open it keeps working with no internet connection at all. When you're happy with a month, you export a colored spreadsheet that looks exactly like what's on your screen, ready to print or hand to the team.

  1. 1 Set up — team, month, roster, commitments.
  2. 2 Solve — one button, one optimal schedule.
  3. 3 Iterate — pin a cell, freeze a date, re-solve.
  4. 4 Export — styled xlsx or print to PDF.

2The 6-day call cycle

Everything the solver does is built around one repeating six-day pattern. Understanding it makes every other panel in this app make sense — read this section even if you skip the rest.

  1. 1 Pre-call
  2. 2 Call
  3. 3 Post-call
  4. 4 Post-post-call (PPC)
  5. 5 Short Call 1 (SC1)
  6. 6 Short Call 2 (SC2) — then the cycle repeats.

Call day

The whole on-service team works. Exactly one person takes night call — an intern, when two or more interns are on service; a team senior instead, for Medicine C teams with no interns on. Everyone else takes day call. No one is "off" on a call day, and there is no pager holder on call days — the whole team is present, so the pager doesn't need a dedicated owner.

Post-call

The person who took night call is the sleeper: they round in the morning, then leave. That rest does not count against their off quota — it's compensation for the night, not a vacation day. The post-call pager goes to the prior day's day-call intern; if there wasn't a day-call intern that day, a working senior holds it instead.

Good to know

A pre-call day that lands on a Tuesday or Thursday is a morning-report day.

Get this right first

The anchor type in Setup tells the app which of these six phases the 1st of the month is, so it can lay the cycle across the whole calendar. Set it wrong and every call day in the month shifts.

3Setup panel — every field

FieldWhat it is & why it matters
Team (A–F) Which inpatient team you're scheduling. Medicine C carries a special senior-only call rule: a team senior can take night call when no interns are on that service.
Month The calendar month. This drives the entire grid — set it first, before anything else.
Anchor type The cycle phase (see §2) that day 1 of the month falls on. If the month starts post-call, you must also fill in carry-in below.
Off quota The target number of counted days off for a resident on service the full month. The app pro-rates this for each resident by their actual service days (rounded half up). Typical value: 4.
Attempt golden weekend A soft goal: give each resident a full Saturday+Sunday off together when the schedule can afford it. Never overrides a hard rule.
Carry-in Appears only when the anchor is post-call. Records who was night, day-call intern, and day-call senior on the last call day of the previous month, so this month's cycle continues correctly from where the last one left off.

4Roster panel

ColumnWhat it is & why it matters
Name The resident's identifier on the grid.
Role Intern or senior — this drives call composition and who is eligible to take night call.
Kind Training background: Family, Transitional, Psych, OB/GYN, or Other. Together with Role it sets each resident's default didactics day (see below).
Start / End The service window — the dates this resident is actually on the team. A resident leaving mid-month gets a shorter window and an automatically pro-rated off quota.
Didactics: day / half / hard The weekly teaching half-day. It's filled in automatically from Role and Kind — seniors and Psych interns Tue PM, Transitional interns Wed PM, Family interns Thu PM — and you can change the day, half, or protection for any resident. Marking it hard makes it protected: the solver only puts them on the pager over hard didactics when there is truly no other option, and it raises a warning when it does.

Add resident and Remove do what they say.

Split seat

The mid-month handoff helper. Use it when one service line needs to be covered by two different people during the month — it duplicates a seat with a replacement resident whose service starts the day after the original resident's ends, and runs through month-end. After splitting, adjust the replacement's dates and didactics to match the real handoff.

5Commitments · PTO · Pins

This panel is a chip editor: choose a mode, then click days directly on a resident's month strip to toggle that mark on or off. Click a chip's × to remove it.

Commitments

Commitment (AM) and Commitment (PM) mark a recurring half-day obligation — clinic, a procedure block, admin time. You'll be prompted for a label. The solver keeps the resident working around it, but a commitment that lands on a call or post-call day is a hard error — fix it with your program admin, since call and post-call can't be split around an outside obligation.

PTO

Marks a vacation or leave day. Like commitments, PTO on a call or post-call day is a hard error — move the PTO to a day that isn't call-adjacent.

The 7 pin types

Pins are your manual override: force a specific assignment for a specific person on a specific day.

PinForces
Off (counted)A day off that counts toward the resident's quota.
Off (free / bonus)An extra day off that does not count against quota — shown as "bonus".
Must workThe resident is working that day.
PagerThe resident holds the pager that day.
Day callThe resident works the call day as day-call, not night.
Night callThe resident is the night-call person that day.
Half off (AM) / Half off (PM)A half day off — counts as 0.5 off, shown as "½ off" on rounders.
Shortcut

You don't have to come back to this panel for every pin — clicking a solved calendar cell creates pins too (see §7).

6Solve

Press Solve to build the optimal schedule for the month. The button is disabled while there are unresolved hard errors — fix those first (see §10). While it runs you'll see a brief "Solving…" state.

No feasible schedule

If the constraints as entered can't be satisfied by any schedule, you'll get a plain-language diagnosis naming the likely culprit pins or constraints. Relax a pin or fix the roster, then re-solve.

After a successful solve you get the full calendar, the totals table, and Potential Issues — soft warnings from an independent auditor that double-checks the solver's work from scratch (see §11).

7Iterating: pins + freeze-through-date

This is the heart of daily use. A first solve is rarely your last — this is how you shape it into the schedule you'll actually ship.

Click a solved cell

Click any OFF, PAGER, or ROUNDERS cell in the solved calendar and a small popover lets you pin that person to an assignment (or clear an existing pin there), then re-solve immediately.

Freeze through

The date box next to Solve locks every assignment on or before that date to the current solution, then re-solves only the remaining days. Use it once the first week or two look right, so tweaking later weeks doesn't reshuffle work you've already approved. Clear the date to free the whole month again.

The loop

Solve → eyeball → freeze the part that's right → pin a fix → re-solve → repeat.

8Reading the calendar, legend, and totals

The calendar lays out in Sun–Sat week blocks. Each day has rows for DATE/TYPE (the cycle phase — CALL is highlighted), ROUNDERS (the day team — call days show "Day - X" / "Night - Y", post-call days are tagged "(postcall)", half-off shows "X - ½ off"), PAGER, CLINIC, DIDACTICS, PTO, and OFF.

Morning Report days

Any pre-call day that falls on a Tuesday or Thursday is a Morning Report day — your team presents. Those TYPE cells are outlined in purple and tagged MORNING REPORT on screen, in the exported xlsx, and in print, so the team can see at a glance which mornings are theirs. The solver already steers days off away from these days; the tag is there so nobody is surprised the night before.

9Exporting & sharing

Export xlsx

A styled spreadsheet whose colors match this grid exactly — drop it into Google Sheets or Excel and hand it to the team. Available once you've solved.

Print

Browser print (→ Save as PDF) of the calendar and totals only — the setup controls are hidden automatically.

Save / Load Scenario (JSON)

Save Scenario downloads the full setup — team, roster, commitments, PTO, pins, and the last solution — as a .json file. Load Scenario reads one back. This is how you carry work between sessions, keep a reusable team template, or share a scenario with a co-chief: send them the JSON, they Load it.

Autosave

The app also autosaves to the browser automatically. The JSON file is your durable, portable copy — the one that survives a new machine or a cleared browser.

Clear scenario

Wipes everything — setup, roster, commitments, PTO, pins, and the solved schedule — back to an empty app, after a confirmation prompt. Use it to start a fresh month from scratch. It also clears the autosave, so Save Scenario first if you might want this month back.

10Errors glossary — hard, block Solve

CodeWhat it meansHow to fix
COMMITMENT_ON_CALL A commitment or clinic half-day lands on a call or post-call day. Move the commitment, or clear it with your program admin.
PTO_ON_CALL PTO lands on a call or post-call day. Move the PTO to a non-call-adjacent day.
PIN_OUTSIDE_WINDOW A pin targets someone outside their service window. Remove or fix the pin, or correct their start/end dates.
NIGHT_PIN_PM_COMMITMENT Someone is pinned to night call but also has a PM commitment that day. Remove one of the two conflicting marks.
CONTRADICTORY_PINS Two pins conflict — e.g. off + must-work the same day, two people both pinned night or pager the same day, an off pin on a call/post-call day, or a day/night pin on a non-call day. Remove one of the conflicting pins.
CARRYIN_REQUIRED The anchor is post-call but carry-in wasn't filled in. Fill in carry-in (see §3).
DEGENERATE_COMPOSITION A call day's on-service roster can't staff call — e.g. no senior on that day, or fewer than two people. Adjust service windows or the roster.

If you ever see this

The app also folds any independent-audit hard-rule violation into this same panel. If you ever see a code starting with A_ here, the solver and the auditor disagree with each other — treat it as a bug and report it.

12Tips & troubleshooting

  • Set Month and Anchor first — a wrong anchor shifts every call day in the month.
  • Fix all Errors before Solve will do anything.
  • Use Load example to see a complete, working month before building your own.
  • Iterate with freeze so approved weeks stay put while you tweak the rest.
  • Colors on screen = colors in the xlsx = colors when you print. One language, everywhere.
  • Everything is local: no data leaves your machine. The JSON scenario file is your save file.
